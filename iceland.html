Mes Pérégrinations

Iceland.

Four seasons in one day, the immensity of this land of fire, water, ice and magnificent colors, swept by the wind. It was my discovery of Iceland.

The midnight sun, what a curious sensation to always have this twilight or this dawn that pursues you throughout the 24 hours of the day. No more marks, but magnificent colors that flood these landscapes. On the summits in the mist, on these endless roads, the strange sensation of being alone in the world.

The land of boiling solfataras, geysers, glaciers, waterfalls and volcanoes is a land of adventure. Its landscapes are spectacular.

Water

My visit to Iceland took me to the Golden Circle and its famous Geysir, which gives its name to the phenomenon of hot water gushing out at more or less regular intervals, to Gullfoss waterfalls and Thingvellir park , whose rift separates the European and American continents. The many volcanoes of the country offer this island a boiling earth and hot springs with odors which notably feed Reykjavik campsite, municipal swimming pools and more.

Thingvellir

Thingvellir Park is located at a meeting point between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates. It looks like a natural amphitheater bordered by its black cliffs, crossed by faults, cracks that lead to the great lake Þingvallavatn.

The immensity...

Take the road N°1 which goes around the island, set off to meet desert landscapes, immense between sea and volcanoes, where water, ice and a mineral landscape coexist.

Get attacked by arctic steres when you cross their nesting place.

On the road

I traveled by public bus, and by hitchhiking.

Hitchhiking works quite well, at least on the most mentioned portions of Route 1, that is to say around Reykjavík, on the side of Mývatn and Jökulsárlón, among others. Otherwise, you can sometimes wait for long time without seeing a vehicle. An advice given to me to wait at a service station, a place visited and ask if there was a free place.

The only problem at the end of the day was to reach a campsite because wild camping is strictly prohibited to safeguard the fauna and flora.
